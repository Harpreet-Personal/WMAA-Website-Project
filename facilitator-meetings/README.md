## Facilitator Meetings
This folder contains Minutes of Meeting notes for facilitator meetings.

### Meeting 01

**Minutes of the Meeting**
- Follow up with the client via email to request an introductory meeting this week.
- Clearly identify and confirm the project goals with the client at the beginning so the proposed website aligns with their expectations and requirements.
- For all upcoming facilitator meetings, prepare the agenda and key discussion points in advance.
- Assign one team member for every meeting to record the minutes.
- Maintain an approach of underpromising and overdelivering throughout the project and in all discussions with the client. Where appropriate, propose additional features that could provide further value to the client beyond the initial scope discussed.
- Develop a thorough understanding of the project requirements and the client’s overall objectives. This should include key areas such as security considerations, language support requirements for the website (English, Chinese, Mandarin, Cantonese, or both where relevant), responsiveness across devices, content expectations, and any specific tools or technologies that need to be used.
- Create a GitHub repository and add all group members, the client, and the facilitator. The repository should serve as the central hub for the project, containing the codebase, design documents, meeting minutes, and all other project-related materials.
- Ensure that the role of project leader rotates every two to three weeks so that responsibility is shared fairly across the team. No single person should carry the majority of the project workload.
- Tasks should be allocated so that at least two team members are involved in each major component of the project. This ensures continuity of work if one member becomes unavailable and also prevents the project from depending too heavily on one person.
- A clear decision-making process should be followed when team members have different approaches to a task. In cases of disagreement, the project leader for that period will make the final decision after considering the team’s input.
- The team should remain adaptable to possible changes in project requirements. Appropriate strategies should be discussed early so that last-stage changes can be managed without significantly affecting the project timeline.
- The team will include reflections at different stages of the project to evaluate progress, capture lessons learned, and identify what worked well and what could be improved.
